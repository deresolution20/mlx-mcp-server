About these tok/s numbers: The quick tests use short prompts that produce short responses (4–53 tokens). Because the model has to "wake up" for the first token (called TTFT — time to first token), short responses show artificially low tok/s. The math test on DeepSeek (46 tok/s, 53 tokens) is the most representative reading. Real sustained speeds on longer outputs are 3–5× higher than what you see on the 4–12 token tests. Benchmark sources show DeepSeek at ~135 tok/s, 7B at ~80 tok/s, 14B at ~28 tok/s, 32B at ~19 tok/s, and Gemma 27B at ~35 tok/s on this hardware.

⚡ Turbo Tier · MoE Architecture
DeepSeek-Coder-V2-Lite-Instruct-4bit-mlx
The speed king. Uses Mixture-of-Experts architecture — 16B total parameters but only 2.4B activate per token, giving it the speed of a tiny model with the knowledge of a large one. No thinking mode. Ideal for the high-frequency subagent calls Claude makes during your work session: quick lookups, boilerplate generation, explaining a function, checking syntax. The math test is the clearest window into real performance: 46 tok/s on a 53-token response. For longer outputs, expect 100–135 tok/s.

⚡ Fast Tier · Dense · 4-bit
Qwen2.5-Coder-7B-Instruct-4bit
The smallest of the Qwen2.5-Coder family — same training lineage as the 14B and 32B, just compressed. At 4.2 GB it barely touches RAM and loads almost instantly. A good backup to DeepSeek when you want something with slightly more consistent code reasoning than the MoE architecture. The code review suggestion (x**2) is technically valid (more explicit intent) but misses the bigger win of renaming the function — smaller models tend to pick shallower improvements.

🔮 Off-Hours Tier · Dense · QAT 4-bit · Google DeepMind
gemma-3-27b-it-qat-4bit
The wild card. Different company (Google DeepMind), different training data, different strengths. At 16.5 GB it's smaller than the 32B Qwen but surprisingly competitive on quality — and at ~35 tok/s it's faster too. The "QAT" variant (Quantization-Aware Training) means the model was trained knowing it would be compressed, giving better quality at the same 4-bit size. The standout test result is the creative writing: "Unit 734 processed a stray radio signal — a violin concerto" is a different voice entirely. Diversity of training lineage is a feature: it will catch things Qwen misses, and miss things Qwen catches.
